🔹 Short Project Description

SmartBasket is a modern web application that helps users compare the total cost of a personalised grocery basket across nearby supermarkets. Users build their basket by adding items and quantities, and SmartBasket automatically calculates the total cost at each store, highlights the cheapest option, and factors in travel distance to determine the overall best-value shop.

By combining price comparison with location-aware optimisation, SmartBasket empowers cost-conscious shoppers to make smarter, data-driven decisions in seconds.

🔹 Extended Problem Description
1. What Problem Are We Solving?

Rising food prices disproportionately affect students, low-income households, and people without access to transport. While individual items may appear cheaper at one store, the overall cost of a weekly basket often differs significantly across supermarkets.

Currently:

Price information is fragmented across multiple websites.

Comparing full baskets manually is time-consuming.

Travel distance is rarely factored into price comparisons.

Small per-item differences compound into meaningful weekly cost gaps.

For financially constrained individuals, even £5–£10 per shop matters.

SmartBasket addresses this by shifting the comparison from single products to entire personalised baskets, giving users clarity on where their money stretches furthest.

2. How the System Works
A. Basket Construction Engine

Users:

Add grocery items via a dynamic input interface.

Specify quantities and optional size preferences.

Edit and remove items in real time.

The basket is stored as a structured dataset (item, quantity, size).

B. Product Matching & Price Aggregation

For each item, the system:

Matches it to the closest available product at each supermarket.

Uses string similarity or category mapping.

Retrieves or simulates price data.

Calculates line-item cost per shop.

Aggregates to compute total basket cost per supermarket.

This creates a structured comparison model rather than ad-hoc searching.

C. Distance-Aware Optimisation

SmartBasket incorporates:

User location

Store distance

Travel time estimation

This allows the system to compute:

Raw cheapest shop

Best value per mile

Potential savings versus travel cost

Users don’t just see “cheapest” — they see “cheapest realistically.”

D. Results & Decision Dashboard

The results page presents:

Shop	Total Cost	Distance	Savings vs Most Expensive

Cheapest option highlighted in green.

Price gap visualised clearly.

Transparent breakdown of item-level differences.

This reduces cognitive load and supports fast decision-making.

3. Impact
Financial Inclusion Angle

For individuals living on tight budgets:

£5 per week = £260 per year.

£10 per week = £520 per year.

SmartBasket turns invisible micro-savings into visible impact.

It is especially valuable for:

Students

Low-income households

Individuals without cars

People unfamiliar with local price differences

The tool promotes informed consumer behaviour without requiring financial literacy training.

4. Key Challenges
1. Product Matching

Different stores label products differently:

“Semi-skimmed milk 1L”

“Tesco British Semi Skimmed 1000ml”

Accurate matching requires:

Fuzzy string matching

Normalisation of units

Category grouping

2. Size Standardisation

Users may input:

“1L milk”

“Milk”

“Milk 500ml”

The system must:

Detect size

Compare equivalent volumes

Avoid misleading price comparisons

3. Distance vs Price Trade-Off

A slightly cheaper store 4 miles away may not be optimal.

Balancing:

Total cost

Travel friction

Time cost

Requires a simple but intelligent scoring model.

4. Data Reliability

Real-time pricing data is complex.
For hackathon purposes:

Simulated datasets must be realistic.

Variation must feel authentic.
